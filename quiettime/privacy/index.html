Privacy Policy for QuietTime

Last updated: 10 April 2026

David Arthur Software ("we", "us", "our") built the QuietTime app ("the App") as a paid application. This page informs you of our policies regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of information when you use the App.

1. Information Collection and Use

QuietTime does NOT transmit any data to us or to any third party. The App has no network code of any kind. Everything you enter or configure is stored only on the device where the App is installed.

The App stores the following data locally on your device:

  • A hashed PIN (your actual PIN is never stored)
  • A hashed recovery password (optional)
  • Schedule settings (block times, weekend days)
  • App whitelist preferences
  • Activity log entries (block/unlock events)
  • User preferences (animation theme, wind-down settings)
  • An optional emergency contact phone number that you enter. This lets a child place an emergency call during quiet hours without needing the parent PIN. The number is stored only on your device, is never transmitted to us or to any third party, and is erased when you uninstall the App.

This data never leaves your device.

2. No Cloud Services

QuietTime operates entirely offline. There are no:

  • User accounts or registration
  • Cloud servers or databases
  • Analytics or tracking services
  • Advertising networks or ad SDKs
  • Third-party data sharing

3. Children's Privacy

QuietTime is designed to be configured by a parent or guardian and used on a child's device. The App does not collect any personal information from children. All settings are protected by a PIN set by the parent.

QuietTime is designed to be safe for use on a child's device under the principles of:

  • COPPA (the US Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) — because QuietTime does not collect, transmit, or share any personal information from children, the obligations COPPA imposes on data collectors do not apply.
  • UK and EU GDPR — the App does not process personal data on our behalf or transmit it to us, so we do not act as a data controller in respect of information entered into the App. You remain in full control of the data on your device.
  • The ICO Age Appropriate Design Code — the App's defaults (PIN-protected settings, minimal data, no tracking, no advertising, no profiling, no nudge techniques) are designed to align with the Code's standards.

4. Permissions

The Android version of QuietTime requests the following permissions:

  • Display over other apps: To show the block screen during quiet hours
  • Usage access: To detect which app is in the foreground
  • Device administrator: To prevent unauthorised uninstallation
  • Receive boot completed: To restart the service after device reboot
  • Foreground service: To keep the schedule monitoring active

The Windows version runs as a standard desktop application and uses:

  • Startup registration: To start with Windows (optional)
  • Foreground window management: To display the block screen

5. Data Security

Your PIN is stored using salted cryptographic hashing and cannot be recovered or read. Schedule and preference data is stored in the app's private storage area, inaccessible to other apps.

6. Data Retention

All data is stored on your device for as long as the App is installed. Uninstalling the App permanently deletes all stored data.

7. Support emails and general correspondence

If you email us directly — for example, to ask for help with the App, report a problem, or ask about our Terms — we will receive your message, including your email address and anything you choose to tell us. We use that information only to respond to you and to keep a brief record of the conversation so that we can help you if you contact us again.

  • We do not add your email address to any marketing list.
  • We do not share your email or its contents with any third party.
  • We keep support emails for up to 2 years, after which we delete them.
  • Our email is handled by standard email infrastructure with encrypted connections.

If you would like us to delete any correspondence you have had with us, email info@davidarthur.app with “Delete my data” in the subject line and we will comply within 30 days. Because our records are small, in practice we can usually do this immediately.

8. Your Rights

Because QuietTime does not transmit or store any of your data outside your device, we (David Arthur Software) do not hold any personal data about you, and there is nothing for us to access, correct, or delete on a server.

Where you need to exercise rights over data stored locally in the App, you can do the following directly on your device:

  • Access / view: open the App and sign in with your PIN to see all stored settings, schedules, whitelists, and the activity log.
  • Rectification: change any stored value in the App's Settings screen.
  • Erasure: uninstall the App, or clear its data from your device's system settings. This permanently deletes everything.
  • Data portability: because no data is held by us in a portable format, this right is not applicable; settings can be manually noted before reinstalling.
  • Object or restrict processing: stop using the App and uninstall it.

If you believe we have handled your data incorrectly and are not happy with our response, you have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk or by phone on 0303 123 1113.

9. Website cookies and local storage

The davidarthur.app website is a simple static site. It does not use analytics, advertising cookies, or tracking of any kind. It does store a small language-preference value in your browser's local storage so that the site can remember the language you selected. This value stays on your device, is never transmitted to us, and can be cleared at any time from your browser's settings.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by updating the “Last updated” date at the top of this policy. For material changes, we will also ask you to re-accept the updated Terms the next time you open the App.

11. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact:

  • Email: info@davidarthur.app
  • Web: https://davidarthur.app
